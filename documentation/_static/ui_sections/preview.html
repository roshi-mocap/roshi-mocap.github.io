#Element
1
Resolution Toggle
Tap to switch between 720p and 1080p video resolution. Affects both preview and recorded video.
2
FPS Toggle
Cycle through frame rates: 30 → 20 → 15 → 10 FPS. Lower FPS gives more processing time per frame for AprilTag detection.
3
Receiver Status
Green dot = connected to LAN receiver. Red dot = disconnected. Tap to open receiver settings (IP/port configuration).
4
Target Detections
Required detections per tag before a recording is considered complete. Cycle: 50 → 100 → 200. Higher = more data for calibration accuracy.
5
Real-time FPS Readout
Shows the actual frames per second being processed. Green color. Helps monitor if the device is keeping up with the target FPS.
6
Tag Detection Tracker
Shows detection progress for all 9 IMU joints. Left (blue): left body joints. Center: pelvis. Right (green): right body joints. Each chip shows the joint name, tag ID, and a progress bar. Colors: gray=idle, red=recording (not yet met target), green=target met.
7
AprilTag AR Overlay
Real-time overlay on detected AprilTags. Shows: green bounding box, 3D axes (red=X, green=Y, blue=Z), and an info panel with Tag ID, distance, and XYZ position in camera coordinates.
8
Zoom Slider
Camera zoom control. Range: 1× to 5×. Use to get a better view of distant AprilTags without physically moving closer.
9
Record Button
Start/stop recording. Red circle = ready to record. Gray = disabled (receiver not connected). When recording, changes to a red square (stop icon). Front camera triggers a 3-second countdown before recording starts.
10
Camera Flip Button
Switch between front and rear camera. Note: front camera uses a countdown before recording.
