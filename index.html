RoSHI enables robust full-body pose estimation from only 9 sparse IMU sensors with AprilTag-based calibration.

Abstract

We present RoSHI, a system for robust human pose estimation using only 9 sparse inertial measurement units (IMUs) placed on the pelvis, shoulders, elbows, hips, and knees. Each IMU is equipped with a rigidly attached AprilTag for automatic calibration of the sensor-to-body-segment rotation offsets.

Our calibration pipeline leverages synchronized AprilTag detections and SMPL/SMPLX body model fitting to estimate per-joint bone-to-sensor rotation offsets. We formulate this as a geodesic averaging problem on SO(3) and support robust M-estimators (Huber, Cauchy, L1) to handle outliers from occlusion, blur, or mirrored tag detections.

Once calibrated, RoSHI reconstructs full-body pose in real-time from IMU readings alone, with per-IMU world frame alignment via AprilTag observations. We demonstrate accurate pose estimation across diverse activities including daily motions and dynamic exercises.

Method

RoSHI operates in two phases: (1) a calibration phase where the subject performs a short motion sequence with AprilTags visible to a camera, and (2) an IMU-only tracking phase where calibrated offsets are applied to live sensor readings for real-time pose reconstruction.
